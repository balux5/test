Physics: The Universal Engine

Physics is the fundamental science that seeks to understand how the universe behaves. It explores the nature of space, time, matter, and energy, providing the building blocks for all other scientific disciplines. From the subatomic particles that make up atoms to the vast clusters of galaxies in the deep cosmos, physics governs every interaction.

In this portal, we focus on the core pillars of physical science. Classical Mechanics, established by Sir Isaac Newton, explains the motion of everyday objects. Thermodynamics deals with heat and energy transfer, which is essential for understanding how engines and biological systems function.

Moreover, modern physics introduces us to Quantum Mechanics and Relativity. These fields challenge our common sense, showing that at very small scales or very high speeds, the universe operates under a different set of rules. Understanding these concepts is vital for the development of future technologies like quantum computers and interstellar travel.

By practicing these tests, you are not just memorizing facts; you are learning to think like a scientist. You are learning to observe, hypothesize, and calculate the very forces that keep the stars shining and the planets in orbit.
